ELIZA
A modern recreation of Joseph Weizenbaum's 1966 ELIZA chatbot, powered by a large language model.
ELIZA was created at MIT to simulate a Rogerian psychotherapist. Weizenbaum was disturbed by how quickly people formed emotional bonds with it — including his own secretary, who asked him to leave the room so she could speak with it privately. He spent the rest of his life warning us about this moment.
This version uses the same deflecting, reflecting style as the original, but with real language understanding underneath — which is exactly what Weizenbaum would have hated.
